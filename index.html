About Me

Greetings!
I am Aimi Azizan, a PhD student at SOKENDAI, conducting my research at the National Astronomical Observatory of Japan (NAOJ). Previously, I completed my master's at the National University of Malaysia (UKM), where I studied the X-ray flares of Sgr A* in the UKM Astrophysics research group. My current research focuses on AGNs and galaxy evolution, and I am a member of the Prime Focus Spectrograph group at the Subaru Telescope.

Research

Sagittarius A* imaged by the Event Horizon Telescope

Chandra's 20-Year View of Sagittarius A*'s X-ray Emissions During Flaring and Quiscent Phases (Past Project):
For my Master's research, I analyzed 20 years of Chandra observations to characterize the quiescent state and flaring events of Sgr A*, examining its X-ray spectral properties such as photon index, column density, and flux over time.
The results was presented at the Asia Pacific Physics Conference (APPC15) and were published here and here.

Detection of Equatorial Plasma Bubbles (EPB) in South East Asia (Past Project):
For my undergraduate thesis, I worked under the supervision of Dr. Shazana Abdul Hamid and detected the presence of EPB over South East Asia. Specifically, I determined the potential of the Bubble Index from ESA's Swarm satellite by comparing the presence of EPB detected by Swarm and ground-based instruments.
The results of this research was presented at Persidangan Fizik Kebangsaan (PERFIK) 2021. The conference abstract can be read here.

The final output from the sunspot detection algorithm that displays the sunspot groups.

Automated Sunspot Number Detection Algorithm (Past Project):
As a research intern at the astrophysics laboratory at Pusat GENIUS@Pintar Negara, I worked on developing a algorithm that can detect and measure the sunspot number automatically using image recognition. The algorithm was tested on several solar images taken from Pusat GENIUS@Pintar Negara, UKM, using a 480mm focal length apochromatic refractor telescope. This study compared the sunspot number obtained from the algorithm with the sunspot number measured manually and with data taken from SILSO.
The results of this research was presented at the Southeast Asia Regional Astronomy Seminar (SARAS) 2021 and the paper can be reached here.
